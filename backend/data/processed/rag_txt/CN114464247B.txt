=== ABSTRACT ===
The application discloses a binding affinity prediction method and device based on antigen and antibody sequences. Wherein the method comprises the following steps: acquiring a first amino acid sequence of a target antigen and a second amino acid sequence of a target antibody, determining initial characteristics of M dimensions according to the first amino acid sequence of the target antigen and the second amino acid sequence of the target antibody, inputting the initial characteristics of M dimensions into a target characteristic fusion model to obtain M fusion characteristics, and inputting the M fusion characteristics into a target prediction neural network model to obtain predicted binding affinity parameters. The application can be at least applied to the field of intelligent medical treatment, and solves the technical problems of complex prediction mode and low prediction efficiency of the binding affinity of antigen and antibody in the related technology.

Protein-protein interaction (PPI) is essential for a wide range of biological activities and functions in the human body, including the immune system. The antibody-antigen system is one of the most important systems among all PPIs. The commonly used antibody affinity determination method is time-consuming and labor-intensive, and has great limitations. It cannot effectively infer the important factors affecting antibody affinity. In recent years, the use of computational-assisted models to predict antibody affinity has become an important breakthrough, but these computational models often require three-dimensional protein structure information or PPI thermodynamic information to be input into the computational model. Considering that this information is still difficult to obtain, it is not suitable for processing large and diverse data sets, resulting in complex prediction methods for antigen-antibody binding affinity in related technologies and low prediction accuracy.

Functional or structural characteristics: The biological activity of protein depends not only on the primary structure of protein molecules, but also on the spatial structure of biological macromolecules that play an important role in life activities. Abnormal spatial structure of proteins is likely to lead to the reduction or loss of their biological activity, and even cause diseases. The study of protein spatial structure is not only conducive to understanding the function of proteins, but also to understanding the biological function of proteins and the interaction between proteins. Any biological cell contains a variety of proteins, each with different structures and special functions. The diversity of their functions is determined by their diverse structures. The primary structure of protein: Protein is a macromolecule composed of one or several polypeptide chains with a certain amino acid sequence. The primary structure of protein refers to the amino acid residue sequence of the protein polypeptide chain. A peptide chain is composed of L-type amino acids arranged in a certain sequence. Adjacent amino acids are condensed to remove a molecule of water to form a peptide bond, which is connected by carbonyl carbon and amide nitrogen. Each protein molecule has its own unique amino acid composition and arrangement order, namely the primary structure. The amino acid arrangement order determines its specific spatial structure, that is, the primary structure of the protein determines the secondary and tertiary structures of the protein. The binding function of the protein, the protein can bind to other molecules or proteins. Specific recognition of other molecules is the key to the function of the protein, which is mainly due to the diversity of protein structure and surface chemical properties. Proteins can serve as components of some major structures in living organisms. The biological function of proteins depends largely on the three-dimensional structural properties of proteins.

Optionally, in this embodiment, the above-mentioned protein structure information file may include but is not limited to a PDB file. The PDB protein structure database (Protein Data Bank, referred to as PDB) is maintained by the Research Collaboratory for Structural Bioinformatics (RCSB). Like the nucleic acid sequence database, data can be submitted directly to the PDB database through the network. The PDB is currently the most important database for collecting 2.5-dimensional (three-dimensional data represented in two-dimensional form) structures of biological macromolecules (proteins, nucleic acids and sugars). It is a three-dimensional structure database of biological macromolecules such as proteins, polysaccharides, nucleic acids, and viruses determined by experimental means such as X-ray single crystal diffraction, nuclear magnetic resonance, and electron diffraction. Its content includes atomic coordinates, references, first-level and second-level structural information of biological macromolecules, as well as crystal structure factors and NMR experimental data, etc. The PDB database allows users to search in various ways and Boolean logic combinations (AND, OR and NOT). The searchable fields include functional category, PDB code, name, author, space group, resolution, source, entry time, molecular formula, references, biological source, etc. The PDB database stores data in the form of text files, with each molecule using a separate file.
